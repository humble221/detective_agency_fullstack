Bug Sweeping & Counter Surveillance

"Bug Sweeping & Counter Surveillance" service by our detective agency is a comprehensive set of technical and tactical measures aimed at detecting and neutralizing hidden surveillance devices.

This service is designed to locate unauthorized eavesdropping equipment, hidden microphones, wiretaps, and concealed cameras in offices, homes, vehicles, and other private areas, safeguarding your business secrets and privacy.

When is Bug Sweeping Required?

Eavesdropping Suspicions

When you have a strong suspicion that your private telephone conversations or business calls are being intercepted.

Confidential Meetings

Before, during, or after hosting high-level confidential corporate negotiations and strategic briefings.

Classified Data Handling

When your daily operations involve managing highly sensitive trade secrets, intellectual property, or financial records.

Office Relocation

Immediately after moving your enterprise into a new corporate headquarters, office building, or private residence.

Information Leaks

If you discover that classified internal data has already been leaked to market competitors or unauthorized parties.

Security Maintenance

As a routine preventative measure to overall enhance and guarantee absolute personal and commercial data security.

What Does the Sweeping Process Include?

Visual Inspection

A rigorous physical sweep of the premises to identify any suspicious, altered objects or hidden spying hardware installations.

RF Signal Analysis

Deploying advanced technical equipment to scan radio frequencies, tracking wireless wiretaps, and signal transmissions.

Non-Linear Detection

Using specialized locators to find electronic components embedded inside walls or furniture, even if devices are turned off.

Acoustic Auditing

Evaluating the entire area for anomalous sounds, ultrasound, or micro-vibrations indicating active listening equipment.

Network Assessment

Auditing local computer networks and landline phone wires to detect deep data leakage channels or tapping implants.

Outcome Report

Compiling a detailed final report with a summary of identified threats, removal results, and future security guidelines.

Advanced Equipment Utilized

• RF Detectors (Radio Frequency): Instruments used to intercept active radio transmissions emitted by hidden wireless spying tools.

• Non-Linear Junction Locators (NLJD): Tools capable of uncovering hidden electronic nodes, whether the bugging hardware is active or dormant.

• Thermal Imaging Cameras: Scanners that visually isolate hidden miniature lenses or recording units by detecting their heat footprints.

• Spectrum Analyzers: Complex hardware modules deployed to evaluate localized RF bands and detect anomalies or frequency hopping bugs.

• Technical Endoscopes: Video probes used to perform deep physical examinations inside complex vents, shafts, and suspended ceilings.
