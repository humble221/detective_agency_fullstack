Asset & Property Trace

"Asset & Property Trace" service by our detective agency is aimed at identifying and locating hidden assets belonging to individuals or legal entities.

It is highly demanded in scenarios involving debt recovery, asset division during divorce, bankruptcy procedures, or asset protection against unlawful actions.

When Might This Service Be Required?

Debt recovery

If a debtor hides assets to evade payments, a detective agency will help locate them and establish legal ownership rights.

Divorce asset division

Divorce processes often involve intense disputes over jointly accumulated property. Detectives help uncover hidden assets that a spouse attempts to conceal.

Bankruptcy cases

During bankruptcy, it is necessary to identify all debtor assets to satisfy creditor claims. We assist in uncovering assets hidden from the bankruptcy trustee.

Fraud investigations

If you fell victim to fraud and lost property, our agency can help establish where it was transferred and who stands behind the scheme.

Inheritance disputes

Inheritance conflicts may require tracing the deceased's property, especially if a significant portion of assets was left out of the official will.

Corporate conflicts

In internal corporate disputes, there might be an urgent need to trace and recover company assets that were unlawfully funneled out of the business.

What Does the Investigation Process Include?

Data Gathering

Detectives compile extensive data on the target/debtor, including their financial transactions, close connections, and proxy owners.

Database Audits

We access specialized registries containing official data regarding real estate, transits, bank accounts, and corporate shares.

Government Requests

Given valid legal grounds, the agency can channel inquiries to state authorities to extract missing asset information.

Physical Surveillance

In specific cases, tactical surveillance helps establish the actual usage and location of movable assets, like vehicles.

Informant Networks

Investigators leverage established relationships with informants to extract insider insights about hidden property.

Financial Analysis

Deep audits of bank statements, crypto logs, and transaction history help trace where funds went and what was purchased.

What Types of Property Can Be Traced?

• Real Estate: Apartments, private houses, land plots, and commercial properties.

• Vehicles: Luxury cars, motorcycles, yachts, and private aircraft.

• Financial Accounts: Active bank accounts, offshore savings, and broker accounts.

• Securities: Stocks, corporate bonds, and investment fund shares.

• Business Shares: Ownership stakes in LLCs and joint-stock company assets.

• Valuables & Art: High-end jewelry, paintings, sculptures, and rare antiques.

• Intellectual Property: Registered patents, trademarks, and copyright assets.
